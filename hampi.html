Hampi

Overview

Hampi is an ancient village in Karnataka, India, that was the last capital of the great Vijayanagara Empire. The ruins of Hampi are spread over 26 square kilometers and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The site is known for its stunning architecture, ancient temples, and unique landscape of boulder-strewn hills.

Quick Facts

  • Period: 14th-16th Century
  • Dynasty: Vijayanagara Empire
  • Style: Dravidian Architecture
  • Location: Karnataka, India

Visiting Hours

  • Open: All days
  • Timing: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Best Season: October to March
  • Entry Fee: ₹30 (Indian), ₹500 (Foreign)

History

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565. The empire was one of the largest and most powerful kingdoms in South India. The city was a major center of trade and culture, attracting merchants and travelers from around the world.

The city was destroyed in 1565 after the Battle of Talikota, when the Deccan Sultanates defeated the Vijayanagara Empire. Today, the ruins of Hampi stand as a testament to the grandeur and architectural brilliance of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Architecture

Hampi's architecture is a unique blend of Dravidian and Indo-Islamic styles. The monuments are built using locally available materials like granite and limestone. The site is known for its massive stone structures, intricate carvings, and innovative water management systems.

Key Monuments

  • Virupaksha Temple
  • Vittala Temple
  • Lotus Mahal
  • Elephant Stables
  • Hazara Rama Temple
  • Queen's Bath

Architectural Features

  • Stone Pillars
  • Musical Pillars
  • Stone Chariots
  • Monolithic Structures
  • Water Channels

How to Reach

By Train

The nearest railway station is Hospet, which is well-connected to major cities in Karnataka. From Hospet, Hampi is about 13 km away. You can take a bus or taxi to reach Hampi.

By Road

Hampi is well-connected by road to major cities in Karnataka. You can reach by bus or private vehicle. The nearest major city is Bangalore, which is about 350 km away.
