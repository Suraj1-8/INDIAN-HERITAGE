Red Fort

Overview

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort in the city of Delhi, India. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years, until 1857. The fort represents the peak of Mughal architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Quick Facts

  • Built: 1639-1648
  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori
  • Style: Mughal Architecture
  • Location: Old Delhi, India

Visiting Hours

  • Open: Tuesday to Sunday
  • Timing: 9:30 AM - 4:30 PM
  • Closed: Mondays
  • Entry Fee: ₹35 (Indian), ₹500 (Foreign)

History

The Red Fort was commissioned by the fifth Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638, when he decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi. The construction was completed in 1648. The fort's name is derived from its massive red sandstone walls.

The fort served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1857, when the last Mughal emperor, Bahadur Shah Zafar, was exiled by the British. The fort was then used as a military garrison by the British Army until India's independence in 1947.

Architecture

The Red Fort is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, combining Persian, Timurid, and Hindu architectural styles. The fort complex covers an area of 254.67 acres and is surrounded by a 2.41-kilometer-long defensive wall.

Key Structures

  • Lahori Gate (Main Entrance)
  • Delhi Gate
  • Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque)
  • Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience)
  • Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience)
  • Rang Mahal (Palace of Colors)

Architectural Features

  • Red Sandstone Walls
  • Marble Inlays
  • Floral Motifs
  • Geometric Patterns
  • Water Channels (Nahr-i-Behisht)

How to Reach

By Metro

Take the Yellow Line to Chandni Chowk Metro Station or Violet Line to Delhi Gate Metro Station. From there, you can take a rickshaw or walk to the Red Fort.

By Road

The Red Fort is easily accessible by road. You can take a taxi or bus to reach the fort. Parking facilities are available nearby.
