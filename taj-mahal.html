Taj Mahal

Overview

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, India. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Quick Facts

  • Built: 1632-1653
  • Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori
  • Style: Mughal Architecture
  • Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Visiting Hours

  • Open: All days except Friday
  • Timing: Sunrise to Sunset
  • Night Viewing: Full moon night and two days before and after
  • Entry Fee: ₹50 (Indian), ₹1100 (Foreign)

History

The Taj Mahal was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. The construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, taking approximately 22 years to build. The emperor was later imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb in the Agra Fort, from where he could see the Taj Mahal until his death in 1666.

The Taj Mahal is not just a tomb, but a symbol of eternal love. It combines elements of Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles, creating a unique and magnificent structure that has captivated visitors for centuries.

Architecture

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its perfect symmetry and exquisite craftsmanship. The main structure is built of white marble, which changes color throughout the day, appearing pinkish in the morning, white during the day, and golden in the moonlight.

Key Features

  • Main Dome (55 meters high)
  • Four Minarets (40 meters high)
  • Reflecting Pool
  • Main Gateway
  • Mosque and Guest House
  • Charbagh (Four Gardens)

Materials Used

  • White Marble
  • Red Sandstone
  • Precious Stones
  • Gold
  • Silver

How to Reach

By Train

Agra is well-connected by train from major cities. The Agra Cantt railway station is the main station, and the Taj Mahal is about 6 km away. You can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw from the station.

By Road

Agra is connected to Delhi and other major cities by the Yamuna Expressway. You can reach by bus or private vehicle. The Taj Mahal is easily accessible from the city center.
