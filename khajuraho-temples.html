Khajuraho Temples

Overview

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Madhya Pradesh, India. Known for their nagara-style architectural symbolism and their erotic sculptures, these temples are among the most beautiful medieval monuments in the country.

Quick Facts

  • Built: 950-1050 CE
  • Dynasty: Chandela Dynasty
  • Architecture: Nagara Style
  • Location: Madhya Pradesh, India

Visiting Hours

  • Open: All days of the week
  • Timing: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM
  • Best Time: October to March
  • Entry Fee: ₹40 (Indian), ₹600 (Foreign)

History

The Khajuraho temples were built during the Chandela dynasty between 950 and 1050 CE. Originally, there were 85 temples spread over 20 square kilometers, of which only 25 temples have survived to the present day.

These temples were lost to the world until 1838 when British engineer T.S. Burt rediscovered them, covered in dense jungle. They represent one of the finest examples of temple art in Northern India, known for their intricate sculptures and architectural brilliance.

Architecture

The temples of Khajuraho are celebrated for their architectural grandeur and intricate sculptures. They are excellent examples of Nagara style architecture and are divided into three groups: Western, Eastern, and Southern.

Notable Temples

  • Kandariya Mahadeva Temple
  • Lakshmana Temple
  • Vishvanatha Temple
  • Chitragupta Temple
  • Parsvanatha Temple

Architectural Features

  • Intricate Sculptures
  • Nagara Style Architecture
  • Shikhara (Spires)
  • Mandapa (Entrance Porches)
  • Detailed Wall Carvings

How to Reach

By Air

The nearest airport is Khajuraho Airport, which is well-connected to major cities like Delhi and Varanasi. The temples are just 2 km from the airport.

By Train

Khajuraho has its own railway station connected to major cities like Delhi, Agra, and Varanasi. Regular trains are available from these cities.

By Road

Khajuraho is well-connected by road to major cities in Madhya Pradesh. Regular buses are available from cities like Jhansi, Orchha, and Bhopal.
